The Lucianic Recension of the Minor Prophets: A New Synthesis

Dimi Septuagint
Published May 12, 2026
Journal of Septuagint Studies, Vol. 14

Abstract

This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the Lucianic fragments discovered in the Cairo Genizah, arguing for a more regional scribal influence than previously estimated. By cross-referencing these fragments with the 4Q122 Deuteronomy scrolls, we establish a pattern of recensional activity that predates the standard Hexaplaric corrections.

Introduction

The problem of the Lucianic (or Antiochene) text has long been at the center of Septuagintal textual criticism. Since Lagarde first attempted to isolate this recension in the late 19th century, scholars have debated its origins, its relation to the Old Greek (OG), and its potential preservation of archaic readings.

In this study, we move beyond the classical framework by utilizing high-resolution multispectral imaging of the Genizah fragments. This allows for a precise palaeographical dating that places the core of these translations in the mid-3rd century CE, significantly earlier than the traditional attribution to Lucian of Samosata.

Key Finding

"The linguistic profile of the Minor Prophets in these fragments suggests a deliberate 'Atticizing' trend that aligns with the broader cultural movements of the Second Sophistic."

Methodology

Our methodology employs a dual-layered approach: first, a quantitative lexical analysis using the new Hellenistic Greek Lexicon project data; and second, a traditional stemmatic comparison with the major uncials (Codex Vaticanus and Codex Sinaiticus).
